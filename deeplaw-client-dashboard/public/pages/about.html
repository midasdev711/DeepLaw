Meet our Founders

Jeremy Spoon, Partner

Jeremy Spoon is an experienced entrepreneur and attorney.

He has significant in-house counsel legal experience under his belt, and already used artificial intelligence to run his mobility startup, Ago, before co-founding DeepLaw.

Before Ago, Jeremy previously worked as a legal technology consultant for a national consulting firm, and worked in legal securities and finance at a small Chicago hedge fund.

Through this experience, Jeremy quickly saw how deep learning could be applied to the legal field and what an advantage and useful tool it could provide to other start-ups and small businesses with on-going legal questions.

Jeremy is graduate of the University of Michigan and Michigan State University College of Law. He is a member of the Illinois State Bar and resides in Chicago.

Ariel Gurian, Partner

Ariel Gurian is a seasoned attorney whose solo private practice prior to co-founding DeepLaw focused primarily on intellectual property, e-commerce, and other start-up and small business needs.

In addition to her legal practice in Chicago, Illinois, Ariel has also worked as a television news journalist for ABC and FOX affiliates, at times balancing both careers simultaneously. The combination of Ariel's media and legal writing skills allows her to train and translate legalese and code to and from the DeepLaw bot, Elaina, into a language easy for clients to understand.

Ariel began her journalism career at the Walter Cronkite School of Journalism at Arizona State University, and her legal career in Loyola University Chicago's JD/MBA program. She is an active member of the Illinois State Bar and currently holds inactive status with the State Bar of Georgia.

In her free-time, Ariel also moonlights for fun as a satirical news anchor for the Onion News Network.
